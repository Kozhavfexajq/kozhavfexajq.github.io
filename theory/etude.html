What is an Étude

Étude is the French word for Study. In music, it is a study, often in a certain technique, often meant to replace finger exercises.

Études are often highly virtuosic (Liszt), and, even though they are based around a technique, are often meant to have a melody.

How to write an Étude

Études are one of the easiest musical forms to write, as you don't need to have a melody, meaning that they are easy to write with writer's block. However, writing them well is still very difficult. I myself am working on my 32nd étude, and several of them have been revised, some multiple times, and many still need to be revised.


To start off, you need a technique. This can be anything from scales, arpeggios, or octaves, all the way to wrist-breaking chords, glissandos, and even phrasing and musicality.

Then, build off of that. For example, if you are writing a study in octaves, you might have a fast melody or chord progression played with octaves, or a slower melody in the right hand and fast octaves in the left.

Form-wise, most Études are in an ABA, AB, or similar format, though some can be Theme and Variations.

Finally, write 11 more. The most common way to write Études are in a set of 12. Composers such as Chopin, Liszt, Alkan, Burgumüller, Hamelin, Moszkowsky, MacDowell, Szymanowky, Debussy, and Scriabin have done this. You don't have to write exactly 12, but it is common. It is still recommended to write more than 1.

Ideas for Études

Exercises

  • Hanon Étude
  • 3rds / 6ths
  • Arpeggios
  • Scales
  • Short theme through keys
    • A short theme, similar to Hanon, that spans 5 or 6 notes, that might go up or down, or through all of the keys.

Traditional Études

  • Study in Arpeggios
  • Study in Speed
  • Study in Octaves
  • Study in Phrasing/Lyricism

Less Traditional Études

  • Chromatics
  • Trills/Double-Notes
  • Study in Rhythm
    • Alkan's Étude WoO in A Minor or Chopin's Trois nouvelles études no. 2
  • Study in another technique
    • Staccato, Portato, Ornamentation, etc.
  • Study in specific numbers of Staves
  • Study on National Themes
  • A Toccata
  • Study on Someone else's works (usually Paganini)
